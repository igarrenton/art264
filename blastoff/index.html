The Exosphere is the uppermost layer of the Earth’s atmosphere. It begins just above the Thermosphere and extends up to half the way to the moon. The air is very thin here, very similar to vacuum of space.

Scientists can’t agree where the exosphere ends and Interplanetary Space begins. Some scientists don’t think the Exosphere exists at all.

The Thermosphere surrounds the Earth at a height of 56 to 600 miles. The air in this layer is very hot, but the pressure here is so high that the many objects that orbit the Earth here never actually get hot enough to burn up.

The Aurora Bourealis occurs in this layer as particles from space collide with particles from the Earth’s Atmosphere.

The Mesosphere surrounds the Earth at a height of 31 to 53 miles. It is the most mysterious layer of the atmosphere because it is too high for planes to fly to, and lower than satelite orbits. Scientists do sometimes use instruments on sounding rockets to sample and study the Mesosphere.

It is very cold in the Mesosphere (-130° F),however, Meteorites
burn up here when they contact the atoms in the Mesosphere.

The Stratosphere starts at about 10 miles in altitude and extends to about 31 miles in height. The air is very thin here, and there is little weather.

This is where the Ozone layer is located, which protects us from harmful UV radiation. The stratosphere gets it’s heat from the Ozone Layer so it actually gets warmer the higher you go in elevation.

The Troposphere is the part of the atmosphere we live in. It is an average of 7 miles high, but it is higher at the equater than at the poles.

This layer is warmer at the bottom than at the top, because it is heated by the Earth’s Surface which absorbs the Sun’s energy.
